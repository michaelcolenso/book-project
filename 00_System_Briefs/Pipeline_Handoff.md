## 2. Key Decisions Made

These are settled. Do not revisit unless the human editor explicitly reopens them.

1. **Audience is Avatar B only.** The Pragmatic Entrepreneur. Analytical, commercially focused, does not need to believe in the product. Every sentence should serve this reader.
2. **Tone is strategic and commercial.** No mystical language except when deconstructing it as a market mechanism. No academic hedging. Direct, mentor-like second person where appropriate.
3. **The ethical bright line is non-negotiable.** Subjective experience and personal transformation only. Never generate text that makes medical, diagnostic, or clinical outcome claims. This is both an ethical principle and a legal risk-mitigation strategy. It is framed assertively, not defensively.
4. **Pipeline discipline: outline before prose.** Every chapter must have a completed beat-sheet in `/02_Chapter_Outlines/` before any prose is drafted in `/03_Raw_Drafts/`. No exceptions.
5. **Research docs are the evidence base.** All chapter outlines and drafts must reference specific data points from `/01_Research_Data/`. No unsupported claims, no invented statistics.

## 9. Style and Formatting Rules

- **Word counts:** Each chapter targets 4,000–6,000 words. Outlines specify per-chapter targets.
- **Tactical asides:** Use second-person direct address for Avatar B insights ("Your competitive advantage is..."). These should feel like margin notes from a mentor.
- **Data anchoring:** Every major claim must reference a specific data point from `/01_Research_Data/`. No floating assertions.
- **Ethical checkpoints:** Every chapter outline includes at least one explicit beat or sub-beat reinforcing the subjective-experience-only boundary. These are structural, not decorative.
- **Chapter opens:** Cold opens only. No "In this chapter, we will explore..." No throat-clearing.
- **Chapter closes:** End on momentum and bridge to the next chapter. The reader should feel pulled forward.
- **Proprietary concepts:** Always reference by name (e.g., "the Legitimacy Gradient," "the Confirmation Engine"). Build vocabulary recognition across chapters.
