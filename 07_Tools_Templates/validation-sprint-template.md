## Week 1 — Verify the Pain
### Objective
Confirm that the pain is real, repeated, and costly enough to matter.

### Tasks
- Collect complaint threads
- Collect money-spent statements
- Collect examples of ugly workarounds
- List trigger moments
- Write one-page opportunity memo

### Output
- Evidence notes:
- Opportunity memo complete? Yes / No

### Success signals
- Repeated pain appears across multiple sources
- Buyers use concrete, frustrated language
- Costs, delays, risk, or lost revenue are visible

### Failure signals
- Feedback is vague or hypothetical
- No clear trigger exists
- Pain sounds “nice to solve” rather than urgent

---

## Week 2 — Test the Offer
### Objective
See whether the buyer responds to a narrow promise.

### Tasks
- Write one-line offer
- Create simple landing page / DM / email offer
- Send outreach to target buyers
- Track replies and objections

### Offer
- What am I offering?
- In one sentence:

### Outreach log
- Messages sent:
- Replies:
- Positive responses:
- Clear objections:

### Success signals
- Buyers ask follow-up questions
- Buyers ask to see it / use it / buy it
- Buyers share workflow details

### Failure signals
- Generic politeness only
- No one cares about the output
- Offer has to be rewritten repeatedly just to get attention

---

## Week 3 — Deliver Manually
### Objective
Deliver the result manually and learn what the buyer actually values.

### Manual version
- Report / checklist / audit / alert feed / spreadsheet / setup:

### Tasks
- Deliver to 1–5 users manually
- Observe which inputs matter
- Observe which outputs matter
- Track where the process breaks

### Output
- What did I deliver?
- What was useful?
- What confused buyers?
- What can be simplified?

### Success signals
- Buyers use the output seriously
- Buyers ask for recurrence
- Buyers provide more data willingly

### Failure signals
- Buyers like the concept but not the output
- Usage is weak
- Nobody wants a repeat version

---

## Week 4 — Decide Brutally
### Objective
Assign a verdict based on evidence, not attachment.

### Questions
- Did I find real pain?
- Did real buyers respond?
- Did anyone pay, commit, or engage seriously?
- Did I learn enough to define v1?
- Is this stronger than my next-best option?
